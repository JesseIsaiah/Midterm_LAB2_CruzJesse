Adobo

Adobo or adobar is the immersion of raw food in a stock composed variously of paprika, oregano, salt, garlic, and vinegar to preserve and enhance its flavor. The Portuguese variant is known as Carne de vinha d'alhos. The term adobo is derived from the Spanish word adobar, meaning marinade. The base of Mexican adobo uses the traditional Spanish spices but adds indigenous ingredients to the mix: tomatoes and chilies. Its only similarity to Spanish and Latin American adobo is the primary use of vinegar and garlic.
